# Pain Points & CX Strategy

---

## Core Pain Points

### 1. Siloed Tools, Siloed Decisions
- Design, Sourcing, Cost, and Quality data live in separate siloed systems with no integration
- Forces the same data to be reviewed repeatedly across teams
- Lack of integration turns design–cost validation into a repeated bottleneck

### 2. Limited BOM Intelligence
- BOM is treated as a static list, not a decision-making asset
- Finding alternative or similar parts requires manual effort
- Limited access to historical pricing and sourcing insights

### 3. Late Cost & Supply Visibility
- Cost and supply impact of design changes is not immediately visible
- Every design revision triggers a full cost revalidation cycle
- Inaccurate target cost estimation in early stage

---

## CX Strategies

### Strategy 1: Contextual Collaboration in Shared BOM Workspace
A shared workspace where cross-functional teams collaborate around a single BOM as the source of truth.
- BOM object–based comments and reviews
- Decision history captured within the BOM workspace

### Strategy 2: Multi-Dimensional BOM Exploration
A structure that enables teams to explore and analyze the BOM across multiple dimensions beyond a static parts list.
- Spec-based Part Search
- Item Similarity Analysis
- Candidate Part Comparison
- Competitive Price Benchmark

### Strategy 3: Shared Impact Visibility & Analysis
A structure that can visualize and analyze the cost and sourcing impact of BOM changes.
- Sub-tier Cost Visibility
- Automated Cost Roll-up
- Cost Comparison (before vs. after)
- Cost Delta Tracking (design version vs. version)
